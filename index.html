bio

Maria Isolina Cozzani (b. 1994, Ludwigsburg) is an Italian-German violinist, composer-performer and multimedia artist based in Cologne. Working at the edges of free improvisation, electro-acoustic performance and installation, she focuses on timbral extremes of the violin, spectral/granular resynthesis and spatial sound (4–8-channel and site-responsive setups). Her practice often integrates audio-reactive objects and upcycled technologies, assembling fragile yet vibrant ecosystems where sound, bodies and space co-shape one another.

She trained in classical and jazz violin at the conservatories of La Spezia and Genoa and completed an MMus at ArtEZ University of the Arts (Arnhem, 2023) with research in free improvisation that culminated in ISLN — Geography of sounds, shapeshifting multiplicities. Recent and ongoing projects include the free-improv quintet Inopia (album Exoplanet, 2025); the electroacoustic duo O.P.M.L. — Our Past Microtonal Love (with Matteo Traverso); and the ensembles Trio Lavish (Erasmus Jazz Prijs 2023) and Edera Trees.

Alongside artistic work, Maria is active as an educator and as a project manager. In 2025 she coordinated the interdisciplinary project week Connecting the Dots at ArtEZ University of the Arts, leading planning and delivery for ~40 Master students across departments, guest-lecturer scheduling, rooming and documentation.

Exoplanet — Inopia (2025)

Album release.

EXOPLANET is a sonic exploration of human existence in an age of global crises. The collective Inopia poses questions of empathy, responsibility, and the relationship between humanity and the environment—expanding the understanding of jazz as a social, emotional, and reflective art form.

The album unites composition and improvisation into an organic whole, merging elements of ambient, drone, electroacoustic music, and free improvisation. Sound becomes a medium of thought and emotion—a mirror of a world in transformation. Sounds interweave, lose their distinct origins, and create new, multilayered spaces of resonance filled with emotional and cultural depth.

Listen: Music-hub · Bandcamp · @inopiasound

what is left, what there grows (2025)

Finalist at MA/IN Matera Intermedia Festival 2025.

“what is left, what there grows” is a self-reactive environment composed of immersive multichannel audio, upcycled trash speakers, small robots, and obsolete technological remains. Although over 90% of the installation is built from discarded or recycled materials, the experience is one of vitality, regeneration, and ongoing transformation.

Art&mide, Studio 1 — The evocation of Ἄρτεμις (2023)

art&mide is an iterative audiovisual installation and performance by Maria Isolina Cozzani & Matteo Traverso. Generative visuals and audio react to movement and sound; the violin’s gestures shape the core image while the synth corrupts it.
